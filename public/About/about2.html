LIBRARY RULES

Library Membership

The College Library membership is open to all bonafide students, teachers, and non-teaching staff of the College. However, the Library card issued to each member is non-transferable and can only be used by the individual to whom it is issued.

Library Timings

The College Library is open five days a week (Monday to Friday). Timings are 9.00 AM to 5.00 Pm excluding Gazetted holidays

Extended Hours: 9 am to 7 pm before and during examinations, from Monday to Friday 9 am to 5 pm before and during examinations, on Saturday.

Admission to the College Library

Upon entering the Library premises, all users must present a valid Identity Card or Library card at the Entrance Gate. Private books and personal belongings, excluding valuable items like money, passport, credit/debit cards, mobile phones, and laptops, are not allowed inside the Library.
Students are required to store their bags and belongings at the Property Counter outside the Library. A token is issued to the students upon depositing their belongings. This token must be surrendered to the Property Counter staff when leaving the Library to retrieve the deposited belongings. In case of losing the token, the user will be subject to paying an admissible fine.

Issue System

Timings: The timings for issuing books are 9:30 am to 1 pm
Duration: General Section Books are issued for two weeks Textbook Section Books are issued for one week and Reference books are only for consultation .The number of books issued to different category of members are as under:
Sno. Member Category No. of Books
1 Pass Course 5
2 Honours Course 5
3 M.A. / M.Sc 5
4 Non-Teaching Staff 5
5 Permanent Teachers 15
6 Ad-hoc Teachers 15

Conditions of Borrowing of Books

Before leaving the counter with an issued book, the user must carefully inspect the book to ensure it is in good condition. If the book is found to be damaged or torn, the user should immediately inform the counter staff to obtain another copy of the book, if available.

Upon returning the book, if any damage or injury to the book is detected, the member will be held responsible for it. The member will be required to either replace the book with the same edition or the latest edition or pay an amount equivalent to the current price of the lost book, along with processing and binding charges. It is essential for users to handle the borrowed books with care to avoid any damages or liabilities.

Loss of Books

The user is responsible for the safe custody of all the books issued to him/her. In the event of loss of a book, the student is required to replace the book either of the same edition or the recent edition to the Library or he/she has to pay an amount equivalent to the current price of the lost book with processing/binding charges.

Loss of Library Membership card / Identity Card

In the event that a student loses or damages their Identity card, they must report the incident in writing to the College Office. After paying the applicable fine, a duplicate Identity Card will be issued to the student. It is important to note that the member will be held responsible for any misuse of the lost or damaged Identity Card.
Certain materials, such as Reference Books, Newspapers, Magazines, Journals, Previous Year Question Papers, Syllabus, and other reference materials, are not available for borrowing under any circumstances. However, users are allowed to consult these materials within the College Library premises.

Overdue Fine

All the books issued on loan have to be returned by the users on or before the Due Date stamped on the Date Slip of the book. In case of late returning of the book, an Overdue Fine @ Rs. 1/- per day per book will be levied on the user.

OPAC KIOSK

OPAC (On-line Public Access Catalogue) is the tool for searching the collection of the Library. This provides facility to search and check the availability of the document/(s) of your choice in the library. The documents could be searched by a number of access points viz. Title, Author, Subject, Publisher, Keywords, etc. These access points could be used individually or in conjunction with each other using Boolean operators AND / OR for searching the documents.
Any User who wants to know more on “How to use OPAC” may contact the Librarian.

Atmosphere of Study

As the Library is a place for individual study, it is necessary to maintain quiet, calm and dignified atmosphere inside it in order to let all the users study without causing disturbance of any sort to the fellow users. Talking and discussion inside the Library are strictly prohibited.

System for Feed-Back

Feed-back register has been placed in the college library wherein the users can give their feed-back and suggestions.
All the suggestions are discussed and analyzed by the Library Committee in its meetings held at regular intervals for further development/improvement.
